Summary

A Marketing Engine Built for a Litigation Firm

Barton Cerjak Law operates across three distinct practice areas — personal injury, commercial litigation, and class action — each with a different buyer, a different decision process, and a different marketing playbook. Most firms treat these as one, which means none of them get the focused, consistent effort they need to compound over time.

This engagement builds the Growth OS: a single AI-powered marketing system that coordinates four specialized sub-agents, each built for one part of the firm's pipeline. The system runs continuously — generating content, maintaining relationships, monitoring case law, and qualifying leads — surfacing only the decisions that require an attorney's judgment. The result is a firm that grows its visibility, its referral network, and its caseload without adding headcount to make it happen.

Strategy

Four Plays. Three Practice Areas.

01
Personal Injury

Own Local Search

PI clients search when they're in crisis — often from a hospital parking lot. The firm that appears first and responds fastest wins. The strategy: dominate local search through a library of hyper-local, intent-rich pages, a meticulously maintained Google Business Profile, and FAQ content structured for AI search and voice queries.

02
Commercial Litigation

Systematize Referral Relationships

Commercial litigation cases come from accountants, bankers, in-house counsel, and other attorneys. These relationships exist — they just aren't being worked systematically. The strategy: map every referral source into a CRM, maintain regular touchpoints with a monthly intelligence brief, and track relationship health so no warm contact goes cold.

03
Class Action

Build Thought Leadership

Class action co-counsel and named plaintiffs vet firms extensively. One substantive article on a novel class certification theory is worth more than fifty generic posts. The strategy: monitor case law continuously, publish original analysis, and place attorneys on the right podcasts and panels — where serious plaintiff-side practitioners are paying attention.

04
All Three Practice Areas

Convert Leads at the Speed of Trust

The single biggest revenue leak in PI firms is intake latency. A prospect in pain calls three firms — the first to respond with genuine, competent engagement wins. The strategy: a 24/7 intake agent that qualifies leads, captures facts, sets appointments, and follows up on unresponsive contacts through a personalized sequence.

The System

One OS. Four Agents.

Orchestrating Layer

The Growth OS

Rather than deploying disconnected tools, the Growth OS is a single super agent that coordinates all four sub-agents. It sets weekly priorities, routes work, monitors performance across practice areas, and surfaces only the decisions that require an attorney's judgment.
